📚 Exam Strategy & Preparation Guide

📋 Exam Pattern & Syllabus Overview

UPSC CAPF AC Exam. Written Exam (Paper 1: GS and Mental Ability MCQ, 250 marks; Paper 2: General Studies, Essay, and Comprehension Descriptive, 200 marks), Physical/Medical Test, and Interview.

📖 Recommended Books List

  • • India's National Security by Kanti Bajpai
  • • Indian Polity by M. Laxmikanth
  • • Geography of India by Majid Husain
  • • Lucent's General Science Reference Book
  • • CAPF AC Solved Papers (Kiran/Arihant)

🔑 Previous Year Paper Tips

Paper 2 requires objective descriptive writing. Practice writing reports, essays, and arguments (for/against) on security, borders, and human rights issues.

🏆 Selection Process Steps

  • 1. Written Examination (Paper 1 MCQ & Paper 2 Descriptive)
  • 2. Physical Standards & Physical Efficiency Test (PET)
  • 3. Medical Standards Examination
  • 4. Personality Test / Board Interview (150 marks)

🎯 Important Topics to Focus

Internal Security Challenges & Border Management, Argument Writing on Socio-Political Laws, Précis and English Grammar, World and Indian Geography, Mental Ability & Data Analysis.

Exam Strategy & Selection Guide

Overview of the stages of selection, syllabus details, and recommended resources.

Syllabus Details

  • CAPF Assistant Commandant Core
  • Border Management Surrounds
  • Internal Security & Counter-Insurgency
  • Argumentative Writing (Paper II)
  • Surveillance & Surrounding Tech
  • Disaster Management & Rescue

Core Selection Tips

  • Justifying troop deployments in simulated border incursions.
  • Contrasting the mandates of BSF, CRPF, and CISF.
  • Explaining the legal scope of AFSPA in conflict zones.
  • Handling a simulated local protest against military camps.
  • Detailing leadership strategies during counter-insurgency operations.

Reference Resources

  • Standard NCERT school textbooks (Class VI - XII)
  • Daily Newspapers: The Hindu or Indian Express
  • Official Government Portals (PIB, Yojana, Kurukshetra)
  • Mock test series papers and past year questions
  • Subject-specific standard reference books
