📚 Exam Strategy & Preparation Guide

📋 Exam Pattern & Syllabus Overview

ISRO / DRDO Recruitment. Written Test (80 core technical questions, objective) followed by a rigorous Technical & Personal Board Interview.

📖 Recommended Books List

  • • Standard branch reference books (e.g. Morris Mano for Digital Design)
  • • GATE Subject-wise Solved Question Banks
  • • ISRO Scientist past papers and solutions compilations

🔑 Previous Year Paper Tips

Focus on gate-level core concepts rather than rote learning. Technical Board interviews heavily test basic fundamental derivations on a whiteboard.

🏆 Selection Process Steps

  • 1. Written Screening Examination (Core Technical MCQ)
  • 2. Technical & Personal Board Interview
  • 3. Medical Examination and Security Clearance
  • 4. Scientist/Engineer 'SC' Post Appointment

🎯 Important Topics to Focus

Engineering Mathematics & Probability, Digital Electronics & Microprocessors, Electromagnetic Theory & Antennas, Computer Architecture & OS, Material Science & Thermodynamics.

Exam Strategy & Selection Guide

Overview of the stages of selection, syllabus details, and recommended resources.

Syllabus Details

  • ISRO Scientist 'SC' Entrance
  • Advanced Engineering Mathematics
  • Orbital Mechanics & Kepler's Laws
  • Rocket & Missile Propulsion Systems
  • Aerospace Control Systems
  • Computational Engineering (CFD)

Core Selection Tips

  • Deriving the orbital velocity equation on a whiteboard.
  • Explaining the differences between solid, liquid, and cryogenic propellants.
  • Designing a closed-loop feedback controller for satellite orientation.
  • Explaining the physical meaning of Maxwell's equations.
  • Detailing boundary layer separation in aerodynamics and how to prevent it.

Reference Resources

  • Standard NCERT school textbooks (Class VI - XII)
  • Daily Newspapers: The Hindu or Indian Express
  • Official Government Portals (PIB, Yojana, Kurukshetra)
  • Mock test series papers and past year questions
  • Subject-specific standard reference books
