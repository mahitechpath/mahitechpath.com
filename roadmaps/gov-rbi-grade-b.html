📚 Exam Strategy & Preparation Guide

📋 Exam Pattern & Syllabus Overview

RBI Grade B Exam. Phase 1 (General Awareness, Quant, English, Reasoning MCQ), Phase 2 (ESI Descriptive/Objective, FM Descriptive/Objective, English Descriptive Typing), and Interview.

📖 Recommended Books List

  • • Indian Economy by Ramesh Singh
  • • Financial Management by Prasanna Chandra
  • • Organizational Behavior by L.M. Prasad
  • • Daily Newspaper: LiveMint or Business Standard
  • • RBI Circulars, Bulletins & FAQ Section

🔑 Previous Year Paper Tips

Typing speed is critical for Phase 2 descriptive papers. Draft answers directly on a computer keyboard. Review ESI and FM topics from government schemes databases.

🏆 Selection Process Steps

  • 1. Phase 1 Online MCQ Test (Sectional Cutoffs apply)
  • 2. Phase 2 Written Descriptive/Objective Online Test
  • 3. Personality Interview (30-marks)
  • 4. Final Merit Ranking & Training Allocation

🎯 Important Topics to Focus

Monetary Policy & Inflation, Banking System Reforms & Basel Norms, Corporate Governance & Leadership Theories, Sustainable Development & SDGs, Fiscal Policy & Union Budget.

Exam Strategy & Selection Guide

Overview of the stages of selection, syllabus details, and recommended resources.

Syllabus Details

  • RBI Grade B Phase 1 & 2
  • Monetary Policy & Macroeconomics
  • Banking Regulation (Basel III)
  • Financial Market Analysis
  • Organizational Behavior & Management
  • Descriptive Business Writing

Core Selection Tips

  • Explaining the transmission mechanism of repo rate changes to retail loans.
  • Analyzing the pros and cons of Central Bank Digital Currency (e-Rupee).
  • Detailing the Basel III capital adequacy ratio requirements.
  • Resolving simulated leadership conflicts in a bank branch environment.
  • Discussing financial inclusion programs like PMJDY.

Reference Resources

  • Standard NCERT school textbooks (Class VI - XII)
  • Daily Newspapers: The Hindu or Indian Express
  • Official Government Portals (PIB, Yojana, Kurukshetra)
  • Mock test series papers and past year questions
  • Subject-specific standard reference books
