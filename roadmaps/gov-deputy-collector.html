📚 Exam Strategy & Preparation Guide

📋 Exam Pattern & Syllabus Overview

State PSC Group 1 Exam. Prelims (MCQ, GS & State History), Mains (Descriptive Papers including Essay, General Studies I-V, and State-specific subjects), and Interview.

📖 Recommended Books List

  • • State Academy Textbooks (History, Culture & Geography)
  • • Indian Polity by M. Laxmikanth
  • • State Economic Survey & Current Budget Publications
  • • Standard Indian History & Geography references

🔑 Previous Year Paper Tips

State-specific topics carry very high weightage in Mains. Memorize facts about local dynasties, state movements, regional geography, and tribal arts.

🏆 Selection Process Steps

  • 1. State PSC Group 1 Prelims (Objective Screening)
  • 2. State PSC Group 1 Mains (5-6 Descriptive Written Papers)
  • 3. State Public Service Commission Board Interview
  • 4. Merit List Ranking & Department Allocation (Deputy Collector, etc.)

🎯 Important Topics to Focus

State History, Movement & Culture, Indian Constitution & Local Governance, Regional Geography & Climate Zones, State Economic Development & Social Welfare Schemes, General Essay.

Exam Strategy & Selection Guide

Overview of the stages of selection, syllabus details, and recommended resources.

Syllabus Details

  • State PSC Group 1 Syllabus
  • Home State History & Geography
  • State Land Revenue Code
  • Panchayati Raj Administration
  • Regional Welfare Schemes
  • Mains Descriptive Writing

Core Selection Tips

  • Detailing the steps to digitize land records in a remote sub-division.
  • Explaining the revenue judicial powers of a Deputy Collector.
  • Resolving a simulated water-sharing dispute between two farmer communities.
  • Discussing the industrial bottlenecks in the home state.
  • Presenting home-district demographic and cultural details.

Reference Resources

  • Standard NCERT school textbooks (Class VI - XII)
  • Daily Newspapers: The Hindu or Indian Express
  • Official Government Portals (PIB, Yojana, Kurukshetra)
  • Mock test series papers and past year questions
  • Subject-specific standard reference books
